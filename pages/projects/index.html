Launch Land Recharge Drone

The Launch Land Recharge (LLR) Drone project aims to develop an autonomously operated drone system that can launch, land, and recharge on a docking station. This drone will play a key role in larger fully autonomous drone systems, providing crucial functionality for rapid deployment in critical environments.

The core feature of the LLR drone is its ability to autonomously locate and return to a docking station, using vision processing to detect April Tags and determine its relative local coordinates. This allows the drone to precisely land on the charging pad after completing its mission, ensuring it is always mission-ready. A fully 3D-printed body and docking station will encapsulate all key components, offering a lightweight, customized solution tailored to the project's requirements.

The drone will support both manual and computer-controlled operation, offering flexibility for a variety of use cases such as disaster response, infrastructure monitoring, and public safety. With robust April Tag detection and processing, it will autonomously hover, land, and launch, making it an integral piece of future automated drone systems.

Autonomous Deterrent and Alert Module

ADAM is an edge device designed to monitor a camera feed, autonomously deploy deterrents, and function as a video doorbell. Its primary role is to provide security by allowing users to monitor and communicate through the device, with a secondary function of autonomously activating a deterrent if necessary.

Key features of ADAM include a 3D-printed housing for all components, a wired connection to power, and wireless communication to a network. The device is self-hosted, offering a web interface for users to view the camera feed, enable or disable autonomous deterrent capabilities, and manually activate deterrents.

The system employs a neural network (NN) to detect specific targets, such as individuals wearing a red hat, and communicates these detections to the website for logging and further action. Users can control deterrent features, and future iterations will enhance functionality by integrating audio streaming, alarm sounds, facial recognition, and additional security layers such as police deterrent lockout features.
